My Story
The support engineer
who thinks in products

Most PMs come from engineering or MBA programs. I came from the front lines of a fintech — a year at FINMO in Bangalore, where I was the person users called when payments broke, onboarding failed, and the product didn't behave the way it promised.

That experience didn't give me less product sense. It gave me disproportionately more of it. I've seen the exact moments where assumptions collapse and users churn. I know what "the product isn't working" actually means at 2 AM during a payment failure.

Before FINMO, I completed a 4-month PM internship where I built the muscle for structured thinking — user research, hypothesis formation, prioritisation frameworks. I also spent 3 months as a management consultant, which trained me to build concise, defensible narratives for stakeholders who don't have time to be wrong.

Now I'm actively completing the Ayuda PM Cohort, doing real product work — not just watching lectures. The case studies in this portfolio are the output of that work: real frameworks, real user interviews, real trade-offs.
